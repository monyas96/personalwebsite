AI Safety & Governance 2025 Co-Creator

VMFS

Verification Mechanism Feasibility Scorer — A decision support tool for AI treaty negotiations

Overview

Despite growing international interest in verifiable AI governance—from the 2023 Bletchley Declaration to the 2025 Paris AI Action Summit—treaty negotiators and policy makers lack a systematic framework for comparing verification mechanisms. VMFS bridges this gap with a multi-dimensional evaluation tool scoring proposals across four dimensions, empowering UN/G7 forums, diplomats, and policy makers to design feasible, halt-capable treaties.

Developed with Apart Research in collaboration with Yatharth Maheshwari, Valeriia Povergo, Jayani Srinivasan, Erik J. Leklem, and Alexandra Moraru.

The Four Scoring Dimensions

Evidence Hardness

"Trust" Score — Is the evidence based on math/physics (Hard) or human trust (Soft)?

Infrastructure Burden

"Cost" Score — Does it require building new hardware (High Burden) or just passing laws (Low Burden)?

Intrusion Burden

"Friction" Score — How much secret data (IP, Weights) is exposed?

Robustness

"Cheating" Score — How easy or hard is it to spoof or hide from this mechanism?

Mechanisms Analyzed

We scored nine verification mechanisms across all four dimensions (1-5 scale), revealing systematic trade-offs:

  • Model Watermarking — Leads overall at 4.1 for low intrusion
  • TEE Attestation — High evidence hardness (5.0) but constrained by infrastructure burden (1.0)
  • Remote Sensing — Average 3.6, good for facility monitoring
  • OPCW-style Declarations — Self-reporting mechanisms
  • On-chip Telemetry — Hardware-based compute monitoring
  • Model Audits — Safety verification at model level
  • Whistleblower Systems — Addressing undeclared activity
  • UN SAB Confidential Verification — Institutional approaches
  • National Technical Means — Remote monitoring (START-inspired)

Key Findings

No silver bullet exists — No mechanism tops 4.1 across all dimensions. Layered portfolios are essential (e.g., remote sensing + audits + whistleblowers).

Trade-offs are systematic — High-hardness mechanisms impose high burden and intrusion, while low-cost mechanisms sacrifice evidentiary strength.

Coverage clusters by evidence location — Audits verify model safety, telemetry tracks compute use, sensing monitors facilities, whistleblowers address undeclared activity.

Application Features

  • Interactive Dashboard — Explore mechanism trade-offs visually
  • Portfolio Sandbox — Construct and test layered verification portfolios
  • Submission Interface — Submit new mechanisms for evaluation
  • Decision Support — Identify coverage gaps for treaty design

Policy Relevance

VMFS enables "halt-capable" governance regimes that can verify compute pauses or unsafe deployments. The tool bridges the policy-tech gap, providing structured decision support for treaty negotiations at UN, G7, and INAI forums. Future work will address Global South dimensions to ensure equity—hardware-heavy methods risk North-South divides without tiered provisions or tech transfer.
