AI-Driven Thematic Analysis of VNRs

Using GPT-based models to analyze Voluntary National Reviews for SDG tracking

Overview

Designed a two-step thematic analysis pipeline integrating Few-Shot Learning, Role-Playing Prompts, and Chain-of-Thought Reasoning using GPT-based models. The methodology was applied to multiple African countries' Voluntary National Reviews (VNRs) to uncover SDG-linked trends and policy blind spots.

What I Built

LLM Pipeline

Two-step thematic analysis combining Few-Shot Learning, Role-Playing Prompts, and Chain-of-Thought Reasoning.

Triple Paradox Lens

Introduced a novel analytical framework based on OSAA's food, energy, and financing paradoxes from the 2023 NEPAD report.

Structured Outputs

Created coding tables linked to SDG indicators, frequency-based word clouds, and co-occurrence matrices.

Power BI Dashboard

Built an interactive dashboard to visualize trends, themes, and comparative SDG progress across countries.

Technical Approach

  • LLM Strategy: GPT-4 with custom prompting strategies for thematic extraction
  • Document Processing: PDF extraction and chunking for VNR documents
  • Analysis: Theme identification, SDG mapping, and temporal trend analysis
  • Visualization: Power BI for interactive exploration of cross-country patterns

Policy Impact

The analysis surfaced policy blind spots in national SDG reporting and identified common challenges across African countries. The Triple Paradox framework provided a new lens for understanding structural barriers to development progress.
