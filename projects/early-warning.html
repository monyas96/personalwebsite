Fragility Analytics & Early Warning 2022–2023 Lead

Early Warning, Early Action

Data-driven early warning systems for fragile and conflict-affected states

Overview

Co-authored with the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Clingendael Institute, this project developed operational frameworks for strengthening data-driven early warning systems in fragile and conflict-affected settings (FCAS). The work demonstrated how to translate complex data into anticipatory governance and policy response.

What I Built

EWEA Data Mapping Tool

Supported the development of a geospatial indicator mapping tool using environmental and conflict data sources.

Climate-Conflict Models

Designed interaction models linking climate stressors (droughts, floods, food insecurity) to conflict dynamics.

Fragility Profiles

Developed context-specific fragility profiles for hotspot identification and priority setting.

Reverse Indicators

Explored the use of recovery potential indicators to improve early response effectiveness.

Technical Approach

  • Geospatial Analysis: Integration of satellite imagery, conflict event data (ACLED), and environmental indicators
  • Indicator Design: Layered, context-specific data frameworks for early warning
  • Climate Modeling: Drought indices, flood risk mapping, and food security proxies
  • Operational Frameworks: Translating technical findings into actionable policy guidance

Policy Impact

The publication informed policy recommendations to the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs and international partners on anticipatory programming and evidence-based risk forecasting. It provided a blueprint for operationalizing EWEA in complex humanitarian settings.
