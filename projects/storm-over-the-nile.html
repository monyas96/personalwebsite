Storm Over the Nile

Computational analysis of Arabic-language polarization in Sudan's conflict discourse

Overview

Co-authored with the Clingendael Institute, this study analyzed how online polarization shaped perceptions of legitimacy, betrayal, and war dynamics during Sudan's 2023 conflict. I led the Arabic-language social media analysis component, building custom NLP pipelines to understand the anatomy of digital political camps.

What I Built

Arabic NLP Pipeline

Custom pipeline for Twitter scraping, cleaning, and analysis specifically designed for Arabic-language content and Sudan-related hashtags.

Network Analysis

Detected influencer clusters, polarized echo chambers, and shifts in hashtag alignment across key political moments.

Sentiment Mapping

Uncovered patterns in emotional tone, framing, and targeting around SAF, RSF, and civil resistance movements.

Disinformation Analysis

Highlighted risks of mis/disinformation, diaspora amplification, and the role of computational tools in conflict monitoring.

Technical Approach

  • Data Collection: Twitter API with targeted hashtag and keyword tracking in Arabic
  • Text Processing: Arabic-specific tokenization, normalization, and stemming
  • Network Analysis: Graph-based community detection and influence mapping
  • Sentiment Analysis: Multi-class classification adapted for Arabic political discourse

Policy Impact

The research informed Clingendael's policy briefs on Sudan, providing evidence-based insights into how digital discourse shapes conflict dynamics. Findings highlighted the need for computational tools in conflict early warning and the risks of unmonitored online polarization.
