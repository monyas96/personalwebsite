Global Value Chains & Livelihoods

Early warning indicators linking trade to fragility and conflict risk

Overview

Co-designed a risk analysis project linking trade, global value chains (GVCs), and livelihoods to detect early warning signals of fragility and conflict. The dataset captures how shifts in production, pricing, and trade patterns translate into risks for workers, inequality, and stability in fragile contexts.

What I Built

Composite Dataset

Combined UN COMTRADE trade flows, input–output tables, and labor market surveys to track employment exposure along value chains.

Inequality Indicators

Designed indicators to detect absolute and horizontal inequalities, disaggregated by region, gender, and formality of work.

Early Warning Logic

Integrated logic where sudden trade shifts (volumes, locations, unit prices) are flagged as livelihood shocks and conflict risks.

Delphi Validation

Validated results through expert consultation to ensure context-specific interpretation of risks.

Technical Approach

  • Data Sources: UN COMTRADE, ILO labor surveys, national input-output tables
  • Indicators: Employment exposure, wage inequality, formality rates, trade concentration
  • Methodology: Value chain mapping with livelihood vulnerability overlays
  • Automation: Scalable model with automated data refresh from open sources

Policy Impact

Applied to fragile contexts where resource access, inequality, and power dynamics drive instability. The framework supports development, humanitarian, and peacebuilding programming by providing early signals of livelihood deterioration before conflict escalation.
